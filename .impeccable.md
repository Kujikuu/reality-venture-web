## Design Context

### Users
Founders applying to venture building and acceleration programs. They're typically early-stage entrepreneurs in the Saudi/MENA region evaluating whether Reality Venture is the right partner for their startup. They arrive with a mix of ambition and uncertainty -- they need to feel confident this platform is serious, credible, and worth their time. Secondary users include consultants listing advisory services and clients booking those consultants, but founders are the primary design target.

### Brand Personality
Modern, trusted, approachable. The interface should feel like a well-run venture firm that's easy to talk to -- not a cold corporate portal, not a scrappy startup page. Prestige without pretension. Innovation without gimmickry.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone**: Premium and clean. Deep purple (#4d3070) paired with gold (#C88B00) anchors the identity -- this palette stays.
- **Typography**: Public Sans (body) + Cairo (Arabic fallback). The current type system works.
- **Theme**: Light mode only. The audience browses during working hours on laptops and phones -- light is the right call.
- **Animation**: Framer Motion is already in use for smooth reveals and tilt effects. Keep animations purposeful and restrained -- they should reinforce credibility, not distract from it.
- **Bilingual**: English and Arabic (RTL) are first-class. Every design decision must work in both directions.
- **Anti-references**: Avoid flashy crypto/Web3 aesthetics, overly playful startup vibes, or dense enterprise dashboards. This is a professional services platform, not a consumer app.

### Design Principles
1. **Credibility first** -- Every pixel should reinforce that Reality Venture is a serious, established partner. No decorative noise that undermines trust.
2. **Clarity over cleverness** -- Founders are evaluating a major decision. Information architecture and copy should be direct and scannable. Don't make them work to understand what you offer.
3. **Warm professionalism** -- Approachable doesn't mean casual. Use generous whitespace, refined typography, and subtle motion to feel welcoming without losing authority.
4. **Bilingual-native** -- RTL isn't an afterthought. Layouts, icons, and interactions must feel equally intentional in both English and Arabic.
5. **Conversion-aware** -- The primary goal is getting founders to apply. Every page should have a clear path toward that action without being pushy about it.
